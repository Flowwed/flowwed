The Gallery page helps you create and manage a beautiful photo album for your guests, allowing you to share memories both before and after your wedding. Upload photos, add short notes, include background music, preview the guest page, and control whether the album is visible on your wedding website.

How to Use This Page

1. Enable the Gallery Page

Use Enable Gallery Webpage to show or hide your Gallery page from guests. Keep it hidden while building the album if needed.

2. Enable Photo Notes

Use Enable Photo Notes to show or hide the notes attached to photos on the Gallery web page your guests will see.

3. Choose a Photo Slot

Select the photo number before uploading. Each image is placed into a specific gallery slot.

4. Upload Photos

Choose an image file and click Upload Photo to Your Page to add it to the wedding gallery.

5. Add a Photo Note

After uploading a photo, you can add a short note to explain the moment behind it. If you skip this step, you'll need to delete the photo and upload it again to add a note.

6. Review Uploaded Photos

The gallery panel displays uploaded photos with their notes, making it easy to review what guests will see.

7. Delete Photos

You can remove any photo from your gallery and replace it with a new one at any time.

8. Add Background Music

Upload an MP3 file to add background music to the gallery page and make the guest experience more personal.

9. Preview Gallery Page

Click Preview Your Page to see how your Gallery will appear to guests on your wedding website.

10. Send Guest Updates

To notify your guests about your new gallery page, use the Update button to open the guest communication tool.

Important Notes

i
You can upload up to 125 photos to your Gallery.
i
Each photo must be under 5MB.
i
Photo notes are limited to 30 words. Keep them short, simple, and meaningful.
i
You'll have the option to add a note immediately after uploading a photo. If you save the photo without a note and later decide to add one, you'll need to delete the photo, upload it again, add the note, and save.
i
Background music must be an MP3 file.
i
In rare cases, heavy server traffic may cause slower page loading. If a page takes longer than a minute to load, simply refresh it and continue your work.
