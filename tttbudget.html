How to Use This Page

1. Set Your Total Budget

Enter the total amount you plan to spend on your wedding. All calculations are based on this number.

2. Review Budget Overview

Monitor Total Budget, Assigned Budget, Unassigned Budget, Total Spent, and Budget Health from the dashboard.

3. Allocate Funds by Category

Assign budgets to categories such as Venue, Catering, Bar, Photography, Flowers, Decor, and more.

4. Add Expenses

Use Add Expense to record deposits, invoices, purchases, vendor fees, and other wedding costs.

5. Track Spending

Every expense automatically updates spending totals and category balances.

6. Monitor Remaining Budget

See how much money remains available in each category after expenses are recorded.

7. Review Budget Health

The Budget Health indicator shows whether your wedding finances are healthy, approaching limits, or over budget.

8. Prevent Overspending

Categories that exceed their assigned budget are highlighted automatically for immediate attention.

9. Review Financial Progress

Progress bars visualize spending levels and help identify categories consuming the largest share of funds.

10. Expand Category Details

Use the expand controls to review larger expense lists and category breakdowns.

11. Update Expenses Anytime

Edit budget amounts and expense records whenever plans, quotes, or invoices change.

12. Download & Print Reports

Export your budget information for planners, family members, venues, or accounting purposes.

13. Automatic Saving

Your budget information is continuously saved as you work, reducing the risk of losing updates.

14. Stay Within Budget

Use category limits, remaining balances, and Budget Health indicators to stay financially on track.

Important Notes

i
Start by entering your Total Budget before assigning category budgets.
i
Assigned Budget should never exceed your Total Budget.
i
Review overspending alerts regularly to avoid unexpected costs.
i
Update expenses immediately after deposits, vendor payments, and purchases for the most accurate forecast.

In short: set your wedding budget, allocate funds, track expenses, monitor financial health, prevent overspending, and keep your entire wedding budget organized from one dashboard.
