How to Use This Page

1. View RSVP Statistics

The ruby summary cards at the top show your live RSVP results: Yes, No, Maybe, and No Response. These numbers help you quickly understand the current status of your guest list.

2. Open Guest Groups

Click any summary card to open the matching guest group below. For example, clicking RSVP Yes displays only the guests who confirmed attendance.

3. Review Guest Details

Each table shows the selected group with guest names, emails, number of guests, meal choices, and messages. This gives you a clear operational view of each RSVP group.

4. Select Guests

Use the checkboxes to select individual guests, or use the master checkbox to select the full group. Guest selection is required before sending emails or SMS reminders.

5. Read Guest Messages

If a guest leaves a message with their RSVP, it appears in the table. Use the Show button to expand longer messages and Hide to collapse them again.

6. Send Email Updates

Select guests and click Send Email. The built-in composer lets you send reminders, schedule updates, venue information, or any wedding-related announcement directly from this page.

7. Send SMS Notifications

Click Send SMS to generate a QR code. Scan it with your phone to quickly send the appropriate reminder or update to the selected RSVP group.

8. Follow Up with Guests

The Maybe and No Response groups are especially useful for follow-ups. Use them to remind guests to confirm their final attendance before your RSVP deadline.

9. Download & Print Reports

Use Download & Print to export any guest group as a CSV file. The export includes guest names, emails, guest counts, meal choices, and messages.

10. Share with Vendors

Exported reports are useful for planners, venues, caterers, seating arrangements, and anyone who needs accurate guest counts or meal information.

11. Monitor Delivery Issues

If an email fails to send, the affected guest is highlighted. This helps you quickly identify delivery problems and follow up manually if needed.

12. Close Guest Tables

Use the close button on an opened table to hide that group and return to the main report view. This keeps the page clean while you move between different RSVP groups.

Important Notes

i
RSVP Reports updates automatically when guests submit their RSVP forms, so your guest statistics and group lists stay current.
i
The RSVP No Response group is one of the most important sections. It helps you quickly identify guests who have not replied and may need a reminder.
i
Before sending emails or SMS notifications, always check that the correct guests are selected in the table.
i
If a delivery issue appears, check the highlighted guest row and resend the message after confirming the guest’s email address or contact details.

In short: use RSVP Reports to monitor attendance, open guest groups, review responses, communicate with guests, export reports, and manage every follow-up from one organized dashboard.
